## Welcome to Redex

Welcome to Redex. We're a logistics and field operations company built on three principles: take care of people, stay accountable, and finish what we start.

Your first week is about getting you set up, meeting the people who will support you, and learning where to find what you need. We don't expect you to memorize anything — we expect you to ask questions, take notes, and lean on your onboarding buddy.

By the end of this module you'll know:
- Who to contact for HR questions.
- Where to find payroll, timekeeping, and PTO information.
- What's expected of you in your first week.
- How to escalate a concern when you need to.

## Who to contact for HR help

Redex People Ops is your first contact for anything HR-related — benefits, pay questions, time off, performance concerns, anything you're not sure about.

- **Primary contact**: People Ops team — peopleops@redex.example
- **Your onboarding buddy**: Sarah Chen — sarah.chen@redex.example
- **After-hours / urgent**: Your direct manager first; People Ops follow-up within one business day.

You always have the right to escalate beyond your direct manager. We'll cover that path in the "Manager escalation" section below.

## Communication expectations

Day-to-day communication at Redex follows three norms:

- **Direct, respectful, factual.** Say what you mean. Avoid ambiguity. If you disagree, say so kindly and bring evidence.
- **Channels match the message.** Slack for quick coordination. Email for documentation and external parties. In-person or video for anything sensitive or difficult.
- **Reply windows are humane.** Standard business hours, target same-day response on Slack and same-week on email. No expectation of after-hours response unless it's an emergency.

You will not be penalized for setting boundaries on after-hours communication. If you feel pressured to be always-on, raise it with your manager or People Ops.

## First-week expectations

A friendly checklist for your first five working days. None of this is graded; it's just a baseline.

### Day 1
- Complete I-9 verification with People Ops (in person, government ID required).
- Sign offer letter and required acknowledgments.
- Set up your laptop / company devices.
- Meet your manager 1:1 for orientation.

### Day 2-3
- Meet your onboarding buddy (Sarah Chen — People Ops).
- Complete the required safety acknowledgments (for field roles).
- Tour your work area / shadow a colleague (role-dependent).

### Day 4-5
- Submit your first timesheet (your manager will walk you through).
- Schedule a 30-day check-in with your manager.
- Complete this onboarding module and any role-specific training assigned.

If anything is unclear or feels rushed, tell your manager OR People Ops. Onboarding is a two-way conversation, not a one-way checklist.
